The psychology behind the choices

The near-black obsidian base is deliberate. It does two things: it creates a gallery-like environment where objects appear to float with luminous depth (as in a museum vitrine), and it signals permanence and seriousness rather than the playful whites of most consumer apps. The warm undertone (#0E0C0A has a slight brown cast versus pure black) prevents the harshness of cold dark UIs â€” it reads as aged ink, not void. The bronze-to-clay gradient of accent colours is drawn from oxidised metal and fired earth â€” materials that collectors intrinsically recognise as valuable and patinated. These tones never feel digital or synthetic. The cream and linen light tones are deliberately off-white (never pure #FFFFFF) â€” pure white reads as clinical and disposable; warm white reads as chosen and cared-for.
